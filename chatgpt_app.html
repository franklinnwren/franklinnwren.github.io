Possible Applications of ChatGPT, and Their Bussiness Visions

Jan 1, 2023 posted by Franklin Ren

Coursework Assistant

Possibility of making money: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Scope of business: ★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Room for start-ups: ★ ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆

Whether you want to admit or not, ChatGPT has overtaken Chegg and become students' favorite coursework assistant. Different from Chegg, answers from ChatGPT could be much more customized to specific questions and unfortunately harder to be detected by anti-cheating tools. After the release of ChatGPT, the web traffic of Chegg has experienced a plummet - maybe it will become the first company killed by ChatGPT.

However, does this field leave room for start-ups? Hardly. A clear reason is that ChatGPT has already satisfied the need of most coursework questions - and even if there are some new questions which is never there before, people could always move to Chegg. Any to-C start-up based on ChatGPT or other LLMs has to be able to provide a superior customer experience than what we currently have, and that could be very hard to achieve unless there is another tech breakthrough.

Grammar Correction

Possibility of making money: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Scope of business: ★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Room for start-ups: ★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Another feature which would be loved by students, and even some company employees, ChatGPT definitely has great potential to overtake Grammarly since its backbone is much more powerful. Certain easy-peasy features could be added to make the grammar correction process smoother.

Are there any room for start-ups? I will give it a two. Very likely OpenAI will be building its own grammar correction tool, but there are still slight chances that a wrapper of ChatGPT or some other LLM specially trained on grammar correction win. Keep in mind this could be a winner-takes-all application. Whoever wins, there will only be one survivor. Just imagine Grammarly, you rarely hear any of its competitors.
